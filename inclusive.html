The original rainbow flag as a whole (pictured on the left) was meant to represent and reflect the diversity of the LGBTQIA+ community, as well as the "spectrum" of human sexuality and gender. Over the years, however, the design of the flag has been modified multiple times.

Each change to the original Pride flag design has been made in response to social events and increasing awareness of the struggles that different members of the community face. The modified flags each aim to take an even stronger stance of inclusion.

Specific examples of modified pride flags include:

  • The Philly Pride Flag, released in 2017 (pictured below to the left)
  • The Progress Pride Flag, released in 2018 (pictured centered below)
  • The Intersex Inclusive Pride Progress Flag, released in 2021 (pictured below to the right)

None of these flags have completely replaced the original Pride flag; rather, they provide additional designs for different members of the LGBTQIA+ community to use as they express themselves and navigate their own identities.
